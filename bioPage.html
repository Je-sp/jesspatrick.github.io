Childhood Years

1 My childhood memories are quite blurry, but I have pictures that remind me of the many places I visited with my family. 2 My parents always tell me stories about how we traveled between provinces almost every year. 3 We frequently rode the RORO (passenger boat) from Batangas to Mindoro, my mother’s hometown. 4 On other occasions, we traveled to La Union, where my father’s hometown is located. 5 My sister, who was working abroad, once surprised us with a trip to Palawan. 6 It was my first time riding an airplane, and I remember feeling both nervous and excited. 7 This happened when I was in fourth grade, making it one of my most unforgettable experiences. 8 Another significant moment in my childhood happened in fifth grade when I joined the Boy Scouts. 9 I was given the honor of carrying the Makati City flag during a school event. 10 By sixth grade, my responsibility grew, and I became the official flag bearer of the Philippine national flag in my school. 11 Throughout various school events, I proudly held the national flag, representing my school, Pembo Elementary School. 12 It felt like an achievement because not everyone was given that role. 13 My elementary school years were filled with excitement and learning. 14 I made many friends and discovered my love for participating in school activities. 15 Despite not remembering everything, the stories and pictures help me reconnect with my childhood. 16 Looking back, I realize how much my early years shaped the person I am today. 17 They taught me the value of travel, family bonding, and leadership. 18 I may not remember every small detail, but the moments I do recall are special. 19 Those experiences helped me become more responsible, confident, and eager to explore the world. 20 Childhood was a phase of joy and innocence, where every new experience brought excitement.

Teenage Years

1 My teenage years were full of new experiences that shaped who I became. 2 From my 7th to 12th grade, I started to explore more about myself and the world around me. 3 It was during high school that I experienced many “firsts.” 4 The first time I had a girlfriend, the first time I learned to play guitar, and the first time I joined a field trip. 5 These were all moments that I will never forget. 6 High school also introduced me to new friendships that I still hold dear today. 7 It was a time where I learned to balance schoolwork with personal interests. 8 I also had my first taste of freedom, where I could hang out with my friends and enjoy new activities. 9 In 11th grade, I attended a school field trip that was free for us, and I made the most of it. 10 I was also introduced to new hobbies, such as playing music and cycling. 11 During the pandemic, cycling became a way for me to cope, and I even rode all the way to Antipolo and Tagaytay. 12 Those long rides, especially the Laguna loop with my friends, gave me a sense of independence and accomplishment. 13 I remember the time I got drunk for the first time, which, although a learning experience, became one of those stories we all laugh about now. 14 The pandemic made me reflect on the important things in life, and I became more aware of the people who truly matter. 15 I also became more responsible, as high school is where I started to understand the importance of balancing work and play. 16 As a teenager, I faced challenges that made me stronger and more determined to chase my dreams. 17 It was during this time that I began thinking about my future more seriously. 18 The teenage years were filled with ups and downs, but they prepared me for the journey ahead. 19 I grew more confident in who I was and learned to navigate the complexities of life. 20 High school gave me the foundation I needed to face the adult world.

College Years

1 Now in my second year of college, studying Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, I look back and realize that choosing this path was not as straightforward as I once thought. 2 In fact, IT wasn’t my first choice; I had initially wanted to pursue a degree in Civil Engineering. 3 However, due to not being accepted into the state universities I applied to, I had to rethink my options. 4 It was a struggle to decide what to do, especially considering the financial challenges of attending a private university. 5 At one point, I even considered not going to college at all, but my sister stepped in and helped me. 6 She promised to pay half of my tuition fees for each school year, making it possible for me to continue my education. 7 My sister also encouraged me to apply for IT, even though I originally wanted to study Computer Science. 8 I trusted her advice and followed her suggestion. 9 When it came to choosing my school, I opted for one far from home, as my sister had recommended it. 10 Looking back, I’m glad I made that choice, as it has provided me with a unique experience. 11 College has been a mix of excitement and anxiety for me, as everything is new. 12 As a slow learner, I sometimes find it frustrating when I can’t grasp things as quickly as I would like. 13 I constantly remind myself to pay attention in class, take notes, and stay focused to improve my learning. 14 This is my first time attending a private school, and the experience has been quite different from my previous years in public schools. 15 I also had my first experience of commuting by train to school, which has become a part of my daily routine. 16 Another first was attending a basketball game as part of my Physical Education subject. 17 Although at first I felt uneasy, it turned out to be a fun and exciting experience. 18 In college, I’ve faced many challenges, but I’ve also had moments of growth and joy. 19 I’m starting to learn to embrace the ups and downs and adjust to this new chapter of my life. 20 I’m excited about what the future holds, and I know that I’ll continue to evolve and learn.
